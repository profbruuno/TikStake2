FAQ

Answers to your questions about TikStake.

What is TikStake?

TikStake is a crypto vault service that locks digital assets until a specified unlock date or proof-of-life deadline, ensuring heirs access holdings only when you’re ready or if you become unresponsive.

Which assets can I lock?

We support major blockchains including Ethereum, Binance Smart Chain, and Polygon. Compatible tokens include ETH, USDC, BNB, MATIC, and any ERC-20 or BEP-20 asset added to our registry.

How do I set an unlock date?

During vault creation you choose an absolute date or a recurring proof-of-life interval. If you miss a required proof, your next-of-kin can activate the vault once the grace period expires.

What proofs are accepted?

Upload a selfie holding today’s newspaper (e.g., August 24, 2025), a timestamped video message, or any other proof that clearly shows the current date. All uploads are encrypted and stored off-chain.

Can I change the unlock date or beneficiaries?

Yes. As long as the vault remains locked, you can log in to modify your proof schedule and add or remove next-of-kin addresses at any time.

Is there a refund or cancellation policy?

Vault fees are non-refundable once the lock period begins. You may cancel or withdraw assets up until your first proof deadline without penalty.

Tutorials

Step-by-step guides to using TikStake.

1. Locking Assets

  • Select the asset you want to lock and click its “Lock Assets” button
  • The platform instantly generates a unique deposit wallet address
  • Send your tokens to that address
  • The wallet address you use to send funds will be the one authorized for future withdrawals

2. Submitting Proof of Life

  • Before each proof deadline, open your dashboard and choose “Submit Proof”
  • Upload a photo or video clearly showing today’s date (e.g., August 24, 2025)
  • You’ll receive an email confirmation once your proof is securely stored off-chain

3. Managing Beneficiaries

  • Navigate to Vault Settings and select the vault you wish to edit
  • Click “Edit Beneficiaries” to add, update, or remove next-of-kin wallet addresses
  • Save your changes; they take effect immediately
